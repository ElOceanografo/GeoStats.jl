Statistics

The term spatial statistics can refer to statistics computed with samples collected in a spatial domain (a.k.a. spatial data), or to statistics computed with multiple realizations of a random field (a.k.a. ensemble).

Data

The following statistics have spatial semantics (i.e. make use of spatial coordinates), and as such, approximate better spatial variables when compared to their non-spatial counterparts:

Statistics.mean — Method
mean(sdata)
mean(sdata, v)
mean(sdata, v, s)

Spatial mean of spatial data sdata. Optionally, specify the variable v and the block side s.

Statistics.var — Method
var(sdata)
var(sdata, v)
var(sdata, v, s)

Spatial variance of spatial data sdata. Optionally, specify the variable v and the block side s.

Statistics.quantile — Method
quantile(sdata, p)
quantile(sdata, v, p)
quantile(sdata, v, p, s)

Spatial quantile of spatial data sdata at probability p. Optionally, specify the variable v and the block side s.

A histogram with spatial semantics is also available where the heights of the bins are readjusted based on the coordinates of the samples (i.e. declustered histogram):

GeoStatsBase.EmpiricalHistogram — Function
EmpiricalHistogram(sdata)

Spatial histogram of spatial data sdata.

Ensemble

A set of geostatistical realizations of a random field represents a probability distribution. It is often useful to compute summary statistics with this set or ensemble:

Statistics.mean — Method
mean(solution)

Mean of simulation solution.

Statistics.var — Method
var(solution)

Variance of simulation solution.

Statistics.quantile — Method
quantile(solution, p)

p-quantile of simulation solution.
